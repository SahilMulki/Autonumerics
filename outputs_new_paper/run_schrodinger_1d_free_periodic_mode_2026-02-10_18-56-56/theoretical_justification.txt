The selected solver employs a spectral spatial discretization with periodic boundary conditions and the ETDRK4 time integrator for the 1D nonlinear Schrödinger equation. This PDE is dispersive, time-dependent, and features smooth, oscillatory solutions that are naturally compatible with spectral methods under periodicity. Spectral schemes are theoretically optimal for such problems, as they provide exponential convergence for smooth solutions, minimizing spatial discretization error and accurately capturing wave propagation and phase information.

The ETDRK4 integrator is specifically designed for stiff, oscillatory, and dispersive equations like the Schrödinger equation. It efficiently handles the linear dispersive term exactly in Fourier space, while treating the nonlinear term explicitly, thus maintaining stability without excessively small time steps. This allows the solver to achieve high accuracy and stability at moderate resolutions (Nx=128), explaining the observed runtime: it reflects the computational cost of repeated FFTs and nonlinear evaluations, but is justified by the stability and accuracy guarantees of the method for this class of PDEs.

In summary, the solver’s success is due to the alignment of spectral accuracy with the PDE’s smooth, periodic structure and the ETDRK4 integrator’s ability to stably and efficiently resolve both linear and nonlinear dynamics inherent to the nonlinear Schrödinger equation.
