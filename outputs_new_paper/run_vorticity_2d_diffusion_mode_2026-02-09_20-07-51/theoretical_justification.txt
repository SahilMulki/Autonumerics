The selected solver employs a finite difference spatial discretization with Crank-Nicolson time integration at high spatial resolution (128×128) for the 2D vorticity equation, a nonlinear parabolic PDE with periodic boundaries. The Crank-Nicolson scheme is unconditionally stable for linear diffusion and remains robust for moderate nonlinearity, allowing for larger time steps without sacrificing stability. The high spatial resolution ensures that fine-scale vorticity structures are well-resolved, which is essential for accurately capturing the evolution of nonlinear features and minimizing numerical dissipation. Periodic boundary conditions are naturally compatible with finite differences and avoid artificial boundary artifacts. Although empirical error and residual metrics are unavailable, the observed runtime is consistent with the computational demands of implicit time-stepping at high resolution, reflecting the cost of solving large linear systems at each step. The solver’s structure thus aligns well with the PDE’s smoothness, periodicity, and parabolic character, supporting both stability and fidelity in the absence of analytic error benchmarks.
