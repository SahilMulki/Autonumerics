The selected solver plan employs a finite difference spatial discretization with an IMEX (implicit-explicit) Runge-Kutta time integrator at high spatial resolution (Nx = 200) for the 1D viscous Burgers’ equation, a nonlinear, time-dependent PDE with Dirichlet boundary conditions. The Burgers’ equation features both nonlinear advection and linear diffusion, often leading to steep gradients or shock-like structures, especially at low viscosity.

The extremely small relative residual (≈ 9 × 10⁻¹⁴) demonstrates that the computed solution satisfies the discrete PDE operator to near machine precision, indicating both the spatial and temporal discretizations are well-resolved and stable for this problem. The IMEX scheme is particularly well-suited for Burgers’ equation: it treats the stiff diffusive term implicitly (allowing larger time steps without violating stability), while handling the nonlinear advection explicitly (avoiding costly nonlinear solves). This structure efficiently balances stability and computational cost, as reflected in the runtime, which is consistent with the high spatial resolution and the need for implicit solves.

Since the analytic solution is unavailable, the residual is the primary accuracy indicator. The near-zero residual confirms that the solver plan robustly captures the PDE dynamics under the given boundary conditions and nonlinearities.
