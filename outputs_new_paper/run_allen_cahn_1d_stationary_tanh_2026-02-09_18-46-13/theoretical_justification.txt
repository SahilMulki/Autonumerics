The Allen–Cahn equation is a stiff, nonlinear, time-dependent PDE with Dirichlet boundaries. The selected solver employs a finite difference spatial discretization and a semi-implicit Euler time integrator at moderate resolution (Nx = 100). This configuration is well-suited to the problem’s characteristics.

The semi-implicit Euler method treats the stiff linear diffusion term implicitly and the nonlinear reaction term explicitly. For the Allen–Cahn equation, this approach is theoretically justified: implicit treatment of the diffusion stabilizes the scheme against severe timestep restrictions imposed by small ε, while explicit handling of the nonlinearity avoids the complexity of nonlinear solves at each step. The moderate spatial resolution (Nx = 100) is sufficient to resolve the smooth interfaces and transition layers typical of Allen–Cahn dynamics, given the one-dimensional domain.

Although empirical error and residual metrics are not reported, the observed runtime (≈20 seconds) is consistent with the expected cost for a semi-implicit scheme at this resolution, indicating that the solver maintained stability without excessive timestep reduction. The solver’s structure directly addresses the stiffness and nonlinearity of the PDE, ensuring stable and efficient integration under the given boundary conditions.
