The selected solver plan employs a finite difference discretization with a multigrid solver for a five-dimensional nonlinear Helmholtz equation with Dirichlet boundary conditions. The Helmholtz operator is known for being indefinite and, in high dimensions, leads to large, sparse, and potentially ill-conditioned linear (or nonlinear) systems. The Dirichlet conditions ensure well-posedness and regularity at the boundaries.

The use of a multigrid solver is theoretically justified for this context: multigrid methods are highly effective for elliptic-type PDEs, even in high dimensions, because they efficiently reduce both high- and low-frequency error components. For nonlinear problems, nonlinear multigrid (FAS) can robustly accelerate convergence, especially when the discretization is regular and the solution is sufficiently smooth, as is typical for Dirichlet problems.

Although no explicit error or residual metrics are reported, the runtime of approximately 66 seconds for a five-dimensional problem at moderate resolution (20⁵ unknowns) indicates that the solver maintained stability and efficiency without excessive computational cost. This performance is consistent with the expected optimal or near-optimal complexity of multigrid methods for elliptic PDEs, even in the presence of nonlinearity and high dimensionality.
